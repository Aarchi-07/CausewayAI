## 🌐 Problem Statement
The challenge revolves around **analyzing large-scale conversational data** (like customer-agent dialogues) to identify **causal triggers** behind important business events such as:
- Escalations to supervisors  
- Refund requests  
- Customer churn signals  

The difficulty is that these events are not caused by a single obvious factor but by **patterns in dialogue** — hesitations, repeated queries, misunderstandings, silences, or branching flows. Current monitoring systems only flag that an event occurred but don’t explain *why*.  

The goal is to build a system that can:
- Ingest large volumes of transcripts (with speaker labels, turn indexing, timings).  
- Detect and extract dialogue spans that causally contribute to events.  
- Provide interpretable, evidence-based explanations.  
- Enable analytic querying across the corpus (e.g., “What conversational patterns lead to escalations in billing discussions?”).  

---

## 📌 Tasks
### **Task 1: Query-Driven Evidence-Based Causal Explanation**
- Accept natural language queries like *“Why are escalations happening?”*.  
- Generate structured causal explanations with supporting evidence from transcripts.  
- Expected outcomes:
  - ML system that outputs responses with evidential references.  
  - Dataset of queries and system outputs.  
  - Defined evaluation metrics for response quality.  

### **Task 2: Conversational Follow-Up and Contextual Response Generation**
- Extend Task 1 to support **iterative dialogue**.  
- Handle follow-up questions while maintaining context.  
- Expected outcomes similar to Task 1, but with conversational continuity.
