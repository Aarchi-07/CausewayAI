Moving from outcome reporting
to causal insight.

Customer support operations generate millions of conversations. Systems today tell you that an escalation happened — not why. CausewayAI tells you why, with evidence, at scale.

01

The problem

Modern contact centres produce enormous volumes of conversational data — speaker-labelled transcripts with turn indexing, timestamps, and outcome flags. But the analytical tools built on top of this data remain fundamentally limited: they report outcomes, not causes.

An escalation is flagged. A churn risk is scored. A refund is processed. But why did the customer escalate? What conversational pattern — a repeated query, a misunderstood policy, an agent's hesitation — was the actual trigger? Current monitoring systems cannot answer this.

The difficulty is structural. Causal triggers are not single-turn events. They are patterns woven across dialogue: hesitations, repeated queries, topic branching, silence after a key phrase. To extract them you must reason across entire transcripts, not individual utterances.

The goal: build a system that can ingest large-scale transcripts, detect and extract dialogue spans that causally contribute to events, and provide interpretable, evidence-based explanations — sustaining multi-turn analytic dialogue with researchers.

02

Our answer

CausewayAI is a dual-retrieval causal reasoning engine. Given a natural-language query — "Why are escalations spiking in billing calls?" — the system retrieves grounded evidence from a 30M+ token corpus and generates a structured, cited explanation.

Two retrieval strategies work in parallel, each optimised for different query types:

02a

GraphRAG — Causal graph traversal

An 88MB knowledge graph encodes calls as nodes, with concepts (topics, outcomes, triggers) as anchor nodes and edges representing co-occurrence, causal adjacency, and domain membership. Given a query, the system resolves relevant concept nodes and traverses the graph — expanding single concepts, intersecting multi-concept neighbourhoods, or finding shortest paths between structurally related regions. This produces a highly faithful, citation-backed answer. Faithfulness: 0.74.

02b

Vector RAG — Semantic prefilter search

A 768-dimensional embedding of every call summary is stored in a Qdrant vector database. Queries are parsed into structured filters — hard-matching domain, soft-matching topic and outcome — then a semantic nearest-neighbour search retrieves the most relevant call segments. Higher recall at the cost of some faithfulness. ID Recall: 36.0%.
